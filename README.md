# IBM Telco Churn: XGBoost + SHAP + Causal Inference

[![Streamlit App](https://static.streamlit.io/badges/streamlit_badge_black_white.svg)](https://telco-churn-xgboost-shap.streamlit.app/)

**Live app:**  **https://telco-churn-xgboost-shap.streamlit.app/**


**Predictive modeling, SHAP explainability, and causal inference on the IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset.**

This project goes beyond a standard churn classifier. It combines three analytical layers to answer three distinct business questions:

| Layer | Question answered |
|-------|------------------|
| XGBoost classifier | Which customers are most likely to churn? |
| SHAP explainability | Why does the model flag a specific customer as high risk? |
| Double Machine Learning | Does a contract upgrade *causally* reduce churn, or do loyal customers self-select? |

## Dataset

**Source:** IBM Sample Data Sets via Kaggle. [IBM Telco Customer Churn](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/yeanzc/telco-customer-churn-ibm-dataset)

7,043 customers, 33 features covering:

- Demographics: gender, senior citizen status, partner, dependents
- Services: phone, internet type, streaming, security, backup, support
- Account: contract type, payment method, paperless billing
- Financial: monthly charges, total charges, CLTV
- Target: churn label (binary), churn score, churn reason

The IBM version includes `Churn Score` and `CLTV` columns not present in most Kaggle versions. These are excluded from modeling (data leakage prevention) but used in analysis.

## Key Findings

### 1. Contract type is the dominant structural driver

Month-to-month customers churn at 42%, versus 11% for one-year and 3% for two-year contracts. This is the single strongest predictor in both the model (top SHAP feature) and the causal analysis.

### 2. The first 12 months are critical

Customers in the 0-12 month cohort churn at approximately 50%. Churn drops steeply after the 24-month mark. Early-tenure onboarding and engagement programs have the highest ROI of any retention initiative.

### 3. The highest-risk compound segment

Month-to-month + Fiber optic + Electronic check customers churn at approximately 3x the overall baseline rate. This specific combination is the primary target for a retention campaign.

### 4. The business is losing its most valuable customers

Churned customers have a higher median CLTV than retained customers. This amplifies the financial impact of churn well beyond what raw counts suggest. Retention prioritisation should weight CLTV, not just churn probability.

### 5. Contract upgrades causally reduce churn

The Double Machine Learning estimate puts the Average Treatment Effect at roughly -0.12 to -0.18: moving a customer from month-to-month to a longer contract causally reduces their churn probability by 12-18 percentage points after controlling for all observed confounders. The effect is strongest for early-tenure and high monthly charge customers.

### 6. Competitor offers, not service failures, drive exit

The top two self-reported churn reasons are competitor-related. Retention strategy must include competitive counter-offers, not just service remediation.
